Originally destined to be hosted via my bedroom using my Raspberry Pi B+ with the ultimate goal of only having to rely on myself to host and develop the website, I briefly hosted the entire site from my RaspberryPi running off the desk in my bedroom, however my ISP didn't like that traffic going in and out (lol), I opted to migrate to GitHub Pages, where I could show off my understanding of the biggest version control system in use today, and to give the Rpi a break and only let it handle my MySQL databases and social media automation.

Twitter Bot!

Enter: Inspirational Arnold. AKA my Twitter bot! I developed Inspirational Arnold purely for fun and laughs, and it has provided me plenty of both. Originally intended to be a project that would help me dive in to the world of APIs and to get a solid understanding of how they work, Inspirational Arnold has helped me do just that. Coded in Python with the use of a few neat libraries that interact with the Twitter API, this bot tweets an inspiring/motivational/funny quote, usually in regards to fitness (I like to lift), at set time intervals. IA follows back everyone who follows him, can unfollow everyone who doesn't reciprocate his follows (on my command) and in the very-near future will re-tweet inspiring fitness quotes from others!

Since his creation, I have sort of just let Arnold loose in to the vast world of Twitter to do as he pleases, and he is slowly accruing followers of his own! Therefore, I basically have a Twitter account that just grows itself... which could one day be pretty useful. I tweak Arnie pretty regularly but for the most part he has an uptime of over 90%.

r/programming's Top Post of the Day!

Another Python project, fun and educational. This script I wrote scrapes Reddit.com/r/programming's top post of the day, every day, saves the post title, the post link, and the author of the post. Once this data has been collected, it is inserted in to a MySQL database (also running off of my RPi!). The data is then called by this website, and displays itself here for me to see. The overall goal of this program was to be able to easily search and save the top posts everyday! Reddit doesn't seem to have an easy way to search for posts day by day... and not browsing every day means that you miss great content... and I'd rather not be obligated to visit everyday when I can just save the content myself using this!
