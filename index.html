Who am I?

I'm a developer from Toronto, Canada. I pursued an education in Computer Science at Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario and in early 2014 I started working professionally on software and web development projects. I feel lucky to belong to (in my opinion!) the first generation to have grown up with the internet. I'm a tinkerer, an athlete, a developer, and I love computers.

I am currently living in New York City, working as a consultant for a number of companies in the tri-state area. I'm so grateful to be able to continue working as a developer while having the opportunity to be here and to work with local devs on fun and rewarding projects.

Lately I have delved further than ever before into my passion for programming -- I love learning new frameworks and languages, discovering their unique aspects, and applying them to fun and rewarding challenges. Abstract and "Legacy" languages are starting to pique my interest, COBOL is next on the list.

What am I doing?

I've been professionally developing since early 2014, working on projects ranging from mobile phone applications, to database management, automation software, and web development. Projects from my personal portfolio can be found here. Some of my most previous work exprience can be found here.

In 2017, I took a more engaging development in creating and conducting an outreach program at Ritsona refugee camp outside of Athens, Greece. I've designed a series of classes where I teach coding basics and fundamentals to those affected by the Syrian refugee crisis. It is both challenging and rewarding, but the opportunity to spread the gift of coding here is a unique experience I'm glad to be living it. Read more about my work at Ritsona here!

In addition to this personal website (a work in progress), I also have a series of personal projects and fun little programs/scripts/things that I make just for fun. You can read about my ever-expanding list of fun/personal development projects I am continually working on here!

Spreading the power of coding at Ritsona Refugee Camp

In 2017 I found myself working with Lighthouse Relief Organizaiton, an NGO heavily involved in helping individuals affected by and victims of the Syrian refugee crisis, at their Greek location, Ritsona refugee camp just north of Athens.

I was the organization's sole developer, working on internal software needs, continued development of the Lighthouserelief.org website and leading coding classes at Ritsona refugee camp.

The experience provided me an opportunity to spread the joys(!) and curiosities of coding while helping in the best way I could, at the end of the day we all deserve the chance to be our best.

Cyber Security/Ethical Hacking
